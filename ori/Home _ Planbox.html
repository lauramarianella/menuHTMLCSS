The challenges

Workforce planning

How might we leverage technologies and data management to provide micro and macro level insights in making cost effective workforce planning decisions that meet short- and long-term business needs?

As the nature of the power industry changes, OPG must ensure that its workforce is optimally positioned, balanced, and empowered in the coming decades. OPG is looking for technologies and data management solutions that will allow them to make insightful, informed, and optimal decisions both at the work execution and work planning levels, across both the micro and macro scales of the business.

OPG’s large workforce requires the use of data to ensure accurate and appropriate decisions and resources are made available to complete tasks and projects as they arise in accordance with OPG’s business plan. This involves careful balancing and optimization of workforce supply curves with respect to the associated demand curves. Information and data on OPG’s workforce come in many different forms – including but not limited to qualification databases, SAP, financial information databases, and timesheets. This information is currently processed using considerable effort, and used individually to make respective planning decisions. OPG Workforce Planning is looking for solutions that can integrate with existing workforce information sources and systems to extract this information and process it such that smarter planning decisions can be made on both a macroscopic and microscopic level. This may involve resourcing within divisions, business units, or facilities and stations as time evolves and the demand curve changes.

Inspection automation

How might we reduce travel time spent on routine inspection activities in remote facilities and deploy workers for higher value tasks?

OPG’s hydroelectric stations are distributed across the province, and often require considerable time and effort to access. Routine visual inspection tasks or simple maintenance tasks carry a higher cost when performed at these stations, as permanent personnel are not stationed at many remote locations. OPG is looking for solutions that will reduce the frequency at which these tasks are done, or the number of personnel needed to perform them. Solutions here will provide critical operational efficiencies to OPG’s hydroelectric fleet.

Hydroelectric generating stations in Ontario can be located in remote regions, particularly in Northern Ontario. These stations are operated remotely, and therefore there are no permanent personnel stationed for inspection and maintenance tasks, which can include things as routine as taking readings from gauges, checking fire extinguishers and fire prevention systems, or performing non-destructive examinations. Such tasks require personnel to be dispatched from the OPG remote operating facilities to these remote sites, which is costly, as a significant amount of time is spent in transit. Additionally, there is always a risk of accidents sending personnel to remote locations. RG at OPG is looking for solutions that may allow for a cost-effective means to perform these routine tasks without dispatching personnel from central monitoring sites. These solutions may involve robotics or other means of remote monitoring, reading, or inspecting.

Data collection in constrained environments

How might we un-obtrusively gather data from fuel channels to confirm ongoing fitness for production?

OPG’s nuclear fleet is composed of 10 operating CANDU reactors – the CANDU reactor is a Canadian design where many fuel channels, essentially two concentric tubes that hold fuel and allow coolant to flow, are arranged to create the reactor core. These fuel channels form critical components of the nuclear reactor, providing the clean and cost-effective energy Ontario relies on every day. The tubes of these fuel channels need to remain concentric and out of contact, a physical condition maintained by spacers located between them. There is a need to inspect fuel channels in an un-obtrusive fashion to ensure the spacers are fit for service, maintaining this critical condition.

Fuel channel inspections are performed by specialized tools delivered into the channel by a delivery machine. These fuel channel tools are designed to be waterproof, resilient to the radiation in the core during shutdown, and durable. In-core radiation fields present challenges for electronics, as non-radiation hardened electronics can experience noise, degradation, and general loss of functionality over time. Fuel channel inspections cover many facets of materials – including pressure tube hydrogen content, flaw characterizing and sizing, and ensuring the pressure tubes remain concentric and out-of-contact. The concentricity of pressure tubes is maintained by spacers, composed of a nickel alloy. These spacers are arranged as circumferential springs wrapped around the inner tube. As the fuel channels age and sag, these spacers ensure that the pressure tube (the inner tube) does not touch the calandria tube (the outer tube). The space between these tubes is filled with circulating carbon dioxide gas as an insulator and means of detecting leaks. Should the tubes contact, they can degrade at an accelerated rate and the fitness for service of the fuel channel is challenged. In order to assess the ability of the fuel channel to withstand such conditions, OPG needs as much information as possible on the state of the fuel channel – this includes material integrity of the inner tube, the spacer itself, ingressed hydrogen fromwater content, and the conditions of flaws and other defects on the channels. The most important piece of information to this mix is the integrity of the spacers themselves. Currently, the integrity of the spacers is assessed by replacing a fuel channel and analyzing the spacers of the old fuel channel – an incredibly expensive effort. As such, OPG is looking for novel technologies that might allow us to infer the integrity of these spacers without replacing a fuel channel.

Automation of monitoring capabilities

How might we leverage emerging intelligent digital technologies to minimize inspection and maintenance downtime of OPG’s critical assets?

OPG performs significant critical asset management services, involving key inspection and maintenance activities across OPG’s generation fleet and beyond. OPG inspects components such as boilers, balance of plant systems, piping, turbines, generators, concrete, and other pieces of infrastructure that form the basis of generating stations. As inspections can often require assets to be taken out of service and result in the unavailability of generating capacity, there is an increasing demand for solutions that allow for the reduction in this downtime. OPG is looking to utilize on-line monitoring capabilities of critical power assets, potentially with data analysis and management, to have smarter, more intelligent inspection programs.

Power generating installations have an array of equipment, from piping, electrical infrastructure and generators, to steam turbines and generators, and gas turbines. This equipment needs to be routinely inspected to ensure that it is safe to operate and can do so reliably until the next inspection. Many of these larger components, such as steam generators and turbines, and smaller components such as valves, electrical buses, and piping, need to be taken out of service for inspections. Any of these inspections can lead to a loss of generating capacity, leading to a loss of revenue and output. There are potentially significant savings and asset availability improvements if these components could be monitored or inspected while online. IRI is looking to leverage digital technologies for online monitoring and inspection of such components to increase the availability of the equipment by avoiding out-of-service inspections, or increasing the effectiveness and efficiency of inspection campaigns performed offline. Solutions collecting and utilizing on-line or off-line inspection data and results, which may allow OPG to make smarter, more effective and optimized inspection planning decisions, would also provide value

How it works

  • 1

    The webinar

    The program launches on September 10 and 11 with a webinar hosted by the MaRS Challenges Team. Each webinar includes a short presentation from each OPG business unit supporting one of the four challenges, followed by a question and answer period.
    Register for the webinar now!

  • 2

    The applications

    Registration through this website opens on September 10. Accepted applicants will be partnered with an OPG mentor, who will assist them in crafting their pitch while providing key details on the challenges as well as the associated OPG business units.

  • 3

    Pitch workshops

    Ventures will then participate in a pitch workshop executed by MaRS that will help them craft the ultimate pitch to OPG’s business unit leadership.

  • 4

    The pitches

    Ventures will participate in a pitch competition event on November 18, where they’ll have a chance to pitch their solution to a panel of judges representing each of the respective OPG business units behind each of the challenges.

  • 5

    Finalist boardroom competition / Winners selected

    Two finalists from each challenge will progress to the boardroom competitions in December. The selected winner will have the opportunity to forge a long-term, mutually beneficial partnership with OPG

Assessment Criteria

Each submission will be reviewed and assessed by the challenge team, who will be asked to rate on a scale of 1 – 5 [where 1 is none and 5 is great] the following:

Impact

To what extent does the organization’s solution have the potential to impact the energy sector beyond OPG?

Alignment

To what extent does the organization’s solution advance the objectives of the applicable OPG business area?

Partnership

To what extent does the organization have a clear vision of how they can partner with OPG?

Feasibility

To what extent is the organization ready to progress in an appropriate timeframe, aligned with OPG’s objectives, based on the current position and scope of their solution?

Value

To what extent is the organization’s solution and associated business model able to generate positive outcomes for OPG, including economic, social, safety and environmental?

Ontario Power Generation produces about half of the electricity Ontarians rely on every day. As the province’s leading clean power generator, Ontario Power Generation produces reliable, low-cost power using our diverse mix of generating sources. With the help of our employees, site communities and partners, we’re also working to ensure a more sustainable future, a cleaner environment, a stronger economy and more livable communities.
OPG continues to invest in new technologies and ideas to make sure we continue to generate safe, clean, reliable power that’s affordable for all Ontarians. As more sectors of the economy, such as transportation, move toward electrification, there will be a growing demand for OPG’s clean power. We are preparing to meet this demand.

OPG is running an innovation challenge in order to connect with the greater venture and start-up community for opportunities to solve some of its business challenges through mutually beneficial relationships, and to continue to innovate and grow as Ontario’s leading clean power generator.

OPG is looking for ventures willing to partner with OPG to co-develop solutions to some of the pressing challenges facing the power industry. This entails not only a short term business engagement, but potentially a long term relationship involving the co-development and commercialization of unique solutions.

The OPG Generate Competition program will connect the winners with talented and engaged personnel providing incredible insights into real problems that OPG is looking to ventures to solve. These connections will allow ventures to develop their solution pitches and value propositions to appeal to the core issues underpinning not only OPG’s challenges, but industry challenges as a whole.

The challenges of the OPG Generate Competition are selected through an analysis of some of OPG’s most pressing business and operational concerns. These challenges have been developed in a partnership with MaRS, and represent the areas where OPG feels that a partnership with ventures can provide the greatest impact.

Ventures that are successful in the competition will receive, in addition to a seed funding prize, access to OPG’s greatest asset – its highly experienced, diverse, cross-functional workforce, which will work with the ventures in a mutually beneficial collaboration to co-develop and implement solutions. This support is part of a relationship that will entail the sharing of information collected over decades of operational experience, procurement support, access to OPG facilities when required, and the technical expertise required to mature solutions for applications at OPG and in the energy and power industries. Beyond this, OPG is looking to form lasting relationships and partnerships with ventures to assist with access to a reliable client and OPG’s development and growth with innovation. Participation in and Winning the 2019 OPG Generate Competition is subject to Terms & Conditions.
